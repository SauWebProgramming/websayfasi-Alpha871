Discover The land of the knowledge, The Fouta Djallon

The Fouta-Djalon almost seems to have been created for hiking, as the paths winding in the shallows, from villages to villages invite to stroll and adventure ... In the heart of Guinea Conakry, it is a massif mountainous area which rises to 1538 meters above sea level and is home to the sources of major rivers such as the Niger, Gambia and Senegal as well as many rivers. A true Eden in Africa, the Fouta Djalon offers us exquisite freshness, incomparable landscapes in West Africa, a very welcoming Fulani population and countless waterfalls that punctuate the landscape, for unforgettable bivouacs and swimming. . Forward for adventure ... The exceptional beauty of its landscapes and the mild climate have consecrated the Fouta Djallon in literature such as African Switzerland, with its plateaus of varied altitudes, its plains and interior depressions, its valleys cashed. The Fouta-Djalon, sometimes spelled Fouta Djallon especially by English speakers, is a mountain range located in Guinea, nicknamed "the water tower of West Africa" ​​because several rivers of West Africa originate there. : Niger, Senegal, Gambia, Koliba. With an area of ​​81,952 km2, and an average altitude of 1,000 m, Fouta-Djalon is a set of plains and hills whose highest point is Mount Loura (1515 m). Indeed, it experiences significant rainfall, and the two main rivers in the region, Gambia and Senegal, have their source there. The plateau consists of a thick sandstone formation that covers the granite rock of the subsoil. Erosion by rain and rivers has carved deep gorges and valleys in the sandstone. The territory is made up of wooded savannas, open forests, gallery forests and vast plains which constitute the typical aspect of the landscape, crisscrossed by numerous rivers whose millennial action of erosion has given rise to large cliffs and many beautiful waterfalls. The climate of Fouta is very pleasant; real African mountain air, temperate and cool. Temperatures vary considerably depending on the location and the seasons which are mainly divided into the dry season (November to May) and the rainy season (June to October)
